## Age-Specific Guidance

### Children & Adolescents (<18)
- **Critical**: Brain still developing
- **Risk**: AI dependency can impact neural development
- **Action**: Limit to 1 hour/day, prioritize learning fundamentals

### Young Adults (18-30)
- **Focus**: Building cognitive skills
- **Risk**: Over-reliance can weaken problem-solving
- **Action**: Balance AI use with independent thinking

### Adults (30-50)
- **Focus**: Maintaining cognitive function
- **Risk**: Gradual cognitive decline acceleration
- **Action**: Regular brain exercises essential

### Seniors (50+)
- **Focus**: Cognitive maintenance
- **Risk**: Higher vulnerability to cognitive decline
- **Action**: Limit AI use, prioritize mental exercises

## Best Practices

1. **Set Daily Limits**: Use AI intentionally, not habitually
2. **Try First, Then AI**: Attempt tasks manually before using AI
3. **Daily Brain Exercises**: Complete at least one suggested task daily
4. **Monitor Trends**: Check cognitive health scores weekly
5. **Balance**: Use AI as enhancement, not replacement for thinking
